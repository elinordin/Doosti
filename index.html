Explore Afghanistan

For centuries Afghan people have welcomed strangers and travelers in their houses. The practice is so deep in the culture that it comes with an old expression: "The guest is God's friend." Guest here means anyone who knocks on one's door regardless of distinctions of race, religion, political affiliation as well as financial status.

Who Are We?

Although Afghanistan being a multinational state didn't ignite the war, but according to most scholars, it fueled the conflict. Therefore, to start the peace process in the country, we must: - Help Afghans to understand people of different ethnicities by exchanging their knowledge, skills, and stories. - Encourage people to form meaningful relationships between their communities.

This is where Doosti comes in. It's a social networking platform based on the above objectives. On this platform, travelers can find local hosts and stay with them. It's a free tool where hosts and users exchange services instead of money. A blend of CouchSurfing and TripAdvisor, it's customized according to the needs of Afghan users.

Since most of the domestic tourism infrastructure like hotels have been destroyed in the war, staying with local people is essential. Doosti will help travelers to find a host in different parts of the country.
